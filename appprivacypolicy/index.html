feral — Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 4, 2026
Contact: jacopo.razza@gmail.com

This is the privacy policy for "feral" (the "App"), an iOS application that
scores freezing of gait from a turn-in-place test. We take a "no surprises"
approach: nothing leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to share
it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. WHAT THE APP ACCESSES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

To do its job, the App accesses the following on the device you install
it on:

  • Rear camera — used to capture short video while you record a session.
    The frames are sent directly to an on-device Core ML model that
    produces per-frame freezing-of-gait probabilities. Frames are NOT
    uploaded to any server.
  • Local storage (the App's sandbox in Application Support) — stores
    the recorded video file, the per-frame scores, the session start/end
    times, and basic device metadata (model identifier, iOS version,
    app version, device name) for each session.

The App does NOT access your photo library, contacts, location,
microphone, calendar, health data, or any other category of personal
information.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2. WHAT LEAVES YOUR DEVICE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

By default: nothing. The App does not connect to any network endpoint
operated by us. There are no analytics, no crash reporters, no advertising
SDKs, and no third-party services bundled in the App.

Data leaves your device ONLY when you explicitly tap one of the export
or share buttons:

  • "Export PDF" on a session card — generates a PDF in a temporary
    location and opens the iOS share sheet. You then choose where it
    goes (AirDrop, Mail, Files, a specific app, etc.).
  • "Export all" → "Human-readable PDF / Machine-readable JSON / Videos
    (ZIP)" — same flow, applied to every saved session.
  • Tapping the share button on a session video in the player.

We are not a recipient of any of this data. Where it ends up is governed
by the privacy policy of whichever destination app you pick.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3. ON-DEVICE INFERENCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The Core ML model that scores your video runs entirely on your iPhone's
Neural Engine and CPU/GPU. It does not require an internet connection
and does not transmit frames or scores anywhere. You can put the device
in airplane mode and the App will still work.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. DEVICE METADATA STORED PER SESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Each saved session includes a small block of device metadata so that a
report exported months later still reflects the device that recorded it:

  • Device model (e.g. "iPhone")
  • Device name (the user-set name, e.g. "Jacopo's iPhone")
  • Device identifier (e.g. "iPhone16,2")
  • iOS system name and version (e.g. "iOS 17.4")
  • App version and build number

This metadata is stored only in the App's local sandbox alongside the
session's predictions and video file. It is included in any report you
explicitly export. We do not transmit it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. DELETING YOUR DATA
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Two controls inside the App fully erase data from your device:

  • Per-session delete (trash button on a session card) — removes that
    session's predictions, metadata, and video file.
  • "Clear all" (toolbar on the Previous Sessions screen) — removes
    every session.

Uninstalling the App also deletes its entire sandbox, including all
saved sessions and videos.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6. CHILDREN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The App is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly
collect any data from them.
